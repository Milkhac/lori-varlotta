The Presidential Transition Partner:

A Key Ally in University Onboarding

Introducing the crucial role of the PTP—an experienced former president who acts as a cartographer and sherpa for the incoming leader.

Who is the PTP?

A recently retired academic president who provides steady, experienced guidance during the formative transition period.

  • Works closely with the incoming leader for approximately three months before and after the start date.
  • Selected by the president-elect, often with board approval.

The Unique Difference

The PTP is an action-oriented partner—a "doer" with no start-up learning curve, unlike traditional coaches or advisors.

  • Active Support: Synthesizes, analyzes, and prepares materials directly.
  • Objective: Can speak candidly without internal political influence.
  • Protective Cover: Provides a neutral context for asking sensitive questions.

What the PTP Is Not

They are a trusted colleague, not a replacement for operational roles.

  • No operational authority or budget control.
  • Not an interim administrator or a long-term executive coach.
  • Does not have line responsibilities or internal reporting duties.

Key Functions: The PTP's Active Role

Curate & Analyze Data

Synthesize complex institutional information, public datasets, accreditation reports, and policies, highlighting critical concerns.

Annotate Financials

Go beyond the raw numbers: annotate budgets and debt summaries, ensuring the new president understands the true financial realities and contexts.

Strategic Roadmapping

Assess the status of strategic planning, advising on engagement and helping plan early cabinet retreats aligned with priorities.

Summarize Governance

Quickly summarize complex governing and operating documents, including bylaws, handbooks, and collective bargaining agreements.

Interpreting Interactions

Serves as a confidential sounding board to interpret "meet and greet" interactions and reframe casual conversations strategically.

Build Board Confidence

Provides trustees reassurance that early presidential decisions are informed by experienced, external guidance.

The Power of the Hybrid Approach

Adding a PTP combines internal support with external expertise, leading to a much stronger transition.

Internal Teams Provide:

  • Knowledge, networks, and operational access.
  • Historical context and institutional relationships.

The PTP Adds:

  • Objectivity, presidential perspective, and actionable insights.
  • An accelerated learning curve and practical guidance.

This strategy ensures the new president’s first months are anchored in collaboration, informed decision-making, and early successes.

PTP: Other Considerations

Selection & Compensation

Chosen by the incoming president (with board approval); often a modest part-time consultancy, sometimes pro bono.

Confidentiality & Ethics

Operates under NDAs and must disclose any potential conflicts of interest.

Scalability

The model could be expanded to support transitions across other executive and institutional roles.

Reference: The Presidential Transition Partner: A New Member of a University President’s Onboarding Team by Lori Varlotta
