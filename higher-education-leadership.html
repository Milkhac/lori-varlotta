Ten Ways Higher Education Leaders Can

Define This Moment

Transformative strategies from Dr. Lori E. Varlotta to create meaningful, lasting change beyond a single campus.

10 Transformative Strategies

1. Prioritize Inquiry Over Activism

Recommit to the foundational purpose of pursuing truth through curiosity and intellectual courage in an age of polarization.

  • Encourage dialogue across differences to counter echo chambers.
  • Support students in hosting diverse speakers on shared topics.
  • Create a “Rapid-Response Fellowship” for scholarly analysis of current events.

2. Combat “Truth Decay”

Address society’s tendency to equate opinion with fact by rebuilding intellectual rigor through disciplined, evidence-based inquiry.

  • Teach how to verify sources and detect disinformation.
  • Model data-informed inquiry in decision-making.
  • Integrate media analysis and fact-checking into academic programs.

3. Seek to Understand, Not Persuade

Foster dialogue and empathy in a divided world by prioritizing genuine understanding over achieving ideological wins.

  • Practice “deep listening” in discussions.
  • Identify and build on areas of common ground.
  • Exchange readings and resources across ideological lines.

4. Practice Democracy through Experiential Learning

Integrate democratic practice directly into education, asserting that democracy must be lived, not merely studied.

  • Incentivize service-learning and community-based research.
  • Recognize civic engagement in tenure and promotion decisions.
  • Build sustained university-community partnerships.

5. Elevate Grassroots Efforts

Amplify student and faculty initiatives that are grounded in thorough research and community relevance, knowing authentic change often comes from within.

  • Host symposia on pressing social and political topics.
  • Fund fellowships for research tied to higher education policy.
  • Reward collaborative, evidence-based activism.

6. Streamline Decision-Making for Agility

Reduce bureaucratic barriers that stifle innovation and ensure universities can act with the urgency needed to remain relevant.

  • Revise governance documents for faster responses.
  • Form rapid-response policy teams.
  • Use Values Impact Assessments (VIAs) to align choices with missions.

7. Diversify and Sustain Funding Sources

Acknowledge that traditional revenue streams are insufficient and advocate for financial resilience through creative partnerships.

  • Expand philanthropic support for academic freedom.
  • Pursue mission-aligned industry collaborations.
  • Explore grants through established vendors and partner organizations.

8. Build Inter-Institutional Coalitions

Focus on collaboration, not competition, to amplify the collective voice of higher education for sector-wide reform.

  • Co-sign statements defending academic freedom and inclusion.
  • Partner on multi-campus advocacy initiatives.
  • Host joint webinars and leadership roundtables.

9. Leverage the Legal System

Use the courts as a critical check on executive overreach and policy threats, upholding the principles of academic freedom.

  • Unite university counsels to evaluate new directives.
  • File or support amicus briefs in key education-related lawsuits.
  • Assert stronger state-level protections for academic freedom.

10. Move Beyond Symbolic Action

Shift from reactionary activism to coordinated, strategic, sequential, and ongoing efforts for lasting impact.

  • Publish a steady stream of op-eds, podcasts, and data-driven visuals.
  • Develop testimony toolkits for legislative advocacy.
  • Partner with local media to share authentic success stories.

Defining Leadership in Higher Education’s Next Chapter

These ten strategies are invitations for leaders to engage both hearts and minds, moving from cautious preservation to courageous innovation. By fostering inquiry, defending truth, and championing collaboration, higher education leaders can do more than respond to change—they can define the next era of higher learning itself.

Reference: Varlotta, L. E. (2025). Ten Transformative Strategies to Navigate the Future of Higher Education.
