Lake Erie College’s Repositioning:

Grounded in Collaboration, Geared for Action

A case study in blending speed with inclusivity to achieve meaningful, campus-wide transformation.

A Partnership Built on Experience and Trust

The initiative was launched by Dr. Lori Varlotta—a two-time college president—demonstrating how transparent leadership can drive change. The process, jointly crafted as an “embedded advisor” model by President Jennifer Schuller and Dr. Varlotta, moved quickly with two intensive, week-long campus engagements in February and May 2025.

Collaborative

Rooted in authentic listening and campus-wide input.

Momentum

Transitioned from launch to final recommendation framework in just three months.

Depth and Breadth of Campus Engagement

Over three months, Dr. Varlotta met with over 100 individuals across 25+ sessions, tailoring the conversation to each audience (e.g., pedagogy with faculty, operations with staff). The key was triangulation—cross-checking themes across groups to distinguish isolated views from systemic patterns.

Contextually Grounded Approach

The repositioning was not a generic script. Dr. Varlotta immersed herself in the College’s life—visiting the Equestrian Center and engaging with the regional context—to build recommendations on what makes Lake Erie distinct.

Defining Strengths Affirmed:

  • A small, close-knit college five miles from Lake Erie.
  • The only four-year institution in Lake County, Ohio.
  • Liberal arts are practiced, not merely studied.
  • NCAA Division II athletics and a nationally recognized equestrian program.

The Outcome: Clarity and Action

The proposals provided a framework for action—not a rigid blueprint. They offer strategic identity statements and programmatic recommendations that celebrate strengths while equipping the College for future challenges with confidence.
