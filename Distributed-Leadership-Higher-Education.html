Dispersed but Not Detached

How Antioch University's leaders build purpose and presence while leading from afar. A model for the future of higher education.

Remote Leadership is Not Remote Control

Effective remote leadership isn't about autopilot. It's about being present in different, intentional ways. Leaders show up consistently across platforms, fostering trust through responsiveness and meaningful engagement, not just physical proximity.

Lead with Values, Not Just Schedules

A shared sense of mission, vision, and values acts as the connective tissue for dispersed teams. A strong, values-based culture centered on social justice and student success is the glue that binds the community across time and space.

Communication is Key

In distributed environments, it's "communication, communication, communication." Strong and timely communication is critical for high-performing teams.

Be Predictable

Establish regular rhythms like weekly updates and monthly reflections.

Be Transparent

Share successes and shortcomings with clarity and honesty.

Be Supportive

Acknowledge great work with a thank-you email or a quick call.

Context Matters—Lead Where You Are

Effective leadership isn't about making every decision. It's about creating conditions for others to lead effectively in their own contexts—whether campus-based or school-based—while aligning efforts with the university's overarching strategy.

Be Visible through Impact

Overcome the challenge of being emotionally in tune without being physically present. Small, personal touches—like handwritten notes, thoughtful check-ins, or public shout-outs—signal attentiveness and show that leading from afar can be a deeply connected practice.

The Future of Leadership is Everywhere

As hybrid work becomes permanent, great leaders will be those who can lead effectively from anywhere, building trust that travels well—across schools, campuses, and the miles between us.
